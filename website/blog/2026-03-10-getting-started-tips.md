Starting a new curriculum at home can feel like a big commitment, but media literacy doesn't have to be complicated. Here are five practical tips to help you and your child get the most out of this program from the very first session.

<!-- truncate -->

## 1. Start with What's Already Around You

You don't need to prepare special materials or print anything fancy. Look around your kitchen table: cereal boxes, junk mail, book covers, grocery store flyers — all of it is media. The very first lesson asks students to spot media in their everyday environment, and you'll be surprised how much there is once you start looking. Let your child lead the hunt. They'll find things you wouldn't have thought of.

## 2. Ask Questions Instead of Lecturing

The most powerful move in this curriculum is asking a question and then genuinely listening to the answer. Instead of explaining what an ad is trying to do, ask your child: *"What do you think they want you to feel when you see this?"* You'll be amazed at how perceptive kids already are. Your job isn't to be the expert — it's to be the curious co-investigator. When you model genuine curiosity, your child will mirror it.

## 3. It's Okay Not to Know All the Answers

This curriculum is designed so that you don't need a background in media studies, journalism, or technology. Some of the best learning moments happen when you and your child figure something out together. If a question comes up that stumps you both, write it down in the Media Detective Notebook and look it up later. Modeling "I don't know — let's find out" is one of the most valuable things you can do.

## 4. Keep Sessions Short and Conversational

Each session is designed to take about 25–30 minutes, and shorter is fine — especially in the early weeks. If your child is engaged and wants to keep going, great. If they're losing focus after 15 minutes, wrap up and come back to it. The goal is to build a habit of thinking critically, not to power through a lesson plan. A good 10-minute conversation about a billboard you passed on the way home can be just as valuable as a full session.

## 5. Use the Media Detective Notebook from Day One

The [Media Detective Notebook](/docs/media-detective-notebook) is a simple journal where students record observations, questions, and reflections throughout the curriculum. Start using it in Week 1, even if entries are just a few sentences or a quick sketch. Over 18 weeks, the notebook becomes a visible record of how your child's thinking has grown — and kids love flipping back to see how much sharper their observations have become.

---

The most important thing is to start. You don't need to be perfect, and you don't need to follow every lesson to the letter. Pick up the curriculum, sit down with your child, and start noticing the media around you together. That's where the learning begins.
