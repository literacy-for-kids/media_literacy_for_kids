Welcome to **Media Literacy for Kids** — an 18-week, open-source curriculum that helps young learners (ages 8–12) think critically about the media they encounter every day. Whether you're a parent, teacher, homeschool educator, or youth leader, this curriculum gives you everything you need to guide meaningful conversations about how media works.

<!-- truncate -->

## What Is This Curriculum?

Media Literacy for Kids is a structured, week-by-week program built for children ages 8–12. Each week includes two guided sessions and one independent creative session — about 80–90 minutes of learning total. The lessons use everyday media (cereal boxes, news headlines, YouTube videos, advertisements) as starting material, so there's almost no prep work required.

The curriculum is organized into five units:

1. **The Anatomy of a Message** (Weeks 1–4) — How media is constructed and why every choice matters.
2. **The Attention Economy** (Weeks 5–8) — How "free" content makes money and what that means for us.
3. **Verification & Debugging** (Weeks 9–11) — How to fact-check, spot fakes, and verify before sharing.
4. **The Algorithmic Echo** (Weeks 12–14) — How algorithms shape what we see and how to broaden our view.
5. **Intentional Production** (Weeks 15–18) — Students create their own media project, applying everything they've learned.

## Our Philosophy

This curriculum is **empowering, not fear-based**. We don't teach kids that the internet is dangerous or that all media is bad. Instead, we help them build a toolkit of questions and habits: *Who made this? Why? What choices did they make? What might I be missing?* The goal is confidence, not anxiety — young people who feel equipped to navigate media thoughtfully rather than avoid it.

## What Makes This Different

- **No expertise required.** Every lesson is scripted with discussion prompts, examples, and caregiver guidance built in.
- **Hands-on from day one.** Students analyze real media in every session and keep a Media Detective Notebook throughout the course.
- **Built for conversation.** The curriculum is designed around dialogue between an adult and a child, not lectures or worksheets.
- **Assessment without tests.** Learning is measured through observation, conversation, and a capstone project — not quizzes.
